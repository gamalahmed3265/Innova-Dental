What Do You Want To Know About Dental Implants?

Are dental implants right for me?

If you’re missing one or more teeth, but otherwise have healthy gums and overall good oral health, you’re probably a good candidate for dental implants.

A dental implant and a crown can replace a missing tooth in a very natural way. If you’re missing several teeth, you may want to consider dental implants with a bridge as a way to hold loose dentures in place more securely.

Even if you’re already wearing dentures, adding two or four dental implants can make the dentures (especially lower dentures) substantially tighter so that they won’t move around while you’re eating or speaking.

I have a medical condition/need to take medication. Can I still get dental implants?

Even if you have a medical condition or need to take medication, you can usually still get implants. However, there are still some things to keep in mind, such as:

Gum Health

Gum disease must be treated before implants. Severe cases may require bone augmentation.

Immune System

Those with weakened immune systems (e.g., HIV or immunosuppressants) may face higher infection risks.

Lifestyle

Heavy smoking or alcohol use can slow healing.

Medications

Anticoagulants (e.g., for strokes/blood pressure) affect bleeding control. Inform your dentist.

Bleeding Tendencies

Hypertension or hemophilia patients may need emergency-ready facilities.

How long does it take to recover after getting dental implants?

Healing time varies, but most practitioners allow 2–6 months for full integration. At LA Dental, we prioritize caution and allow ample healing time before placing restorations.

Are all dental implants the same?

No! Implants vary by design (one-piece vs. two-piece) and material (metal vs. ceramic). We’ll explain options during your consultation.

What’s the difference between a crown and an implant?

The "implant" refers to the screw anchored into the jaw. The "crown" is the visible tooth-like restoration attached to the implant. They are separate components.
