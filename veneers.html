What to expect.

1st consultation

We’ll take photos and scans of your teeth, then talk about your smile goals and check out different teeth shapes to see what looks good on you.

Step 2: 3D mock-up

Once we know what you want, we digitally design and fit veneers for your teeth, then print a 3D model to show you how your veneers will look.

Step 3: 2nd consultation

Together, we’ll review the digital and printed mock-ups of your smile. We’ll make changes until you’re happy with it, then move on to tooth prep.

Step 4: Teeth prep

Next, you’ll come in to have your teeth prepared. That means shaping and adjustment, tooth color matching, plus a few more digital impressions and photos. You’ll also receive temporary veneers based on your original mock-up. One thing to know: we use a biomimetic, minimally invasive approach, so we only remove tooth structure if it’s absolutely necessary.

Step 5: Temporary check

A few days after your prep appointment, you’ll come in again to let us know how your temporaries are looking and feeling. We’ll let our lab know about any changes you’d like to make or if you’re happy with the current shape.

Step 6: Veneers fitting

Once your veneers are ready, you’ll return to have them fitted in place of your temporaries. We’ll make sure they fit well, have the right shape and color, and that you’re happy with how they look. If everything checks out, they’ll be bonded to your teeth, adjusted, and polished.

Step 7: Final check

A few days after your fitting you’ll come back for a final appointment. We’ll check the veneers, make sure they’re secure, polish them one more time, and take final photos.

How much do veneers cost in Los Angeles?

Our veneers start at $1000 per veneer, but at most places in LA you can expect to pay between $1200 and $2500 per veneer.

Do veneers ruin your teeth?

No, veneers don’t ruin your teeth. Tooth enamel doesn’t need to be removed in every case, and even when it does, very little gets taken off – about the thickness of your fingernail.

Are veneers permanent?

Veneers look and feel like natural, permanent teeth, but they don’t last forever. If you take good care of them, though, they’ll last for at least 15-20 years.

Do you brush with veneers?

Yes, you still need to floss and brush your teeth when you have veneers.

What’s the difference between composite and porcelain veneers?

Compared to composite veneers, porcelain veneers are the premium option – they last longer, are more durable, and are virtually stain-proof… but also cost more.
